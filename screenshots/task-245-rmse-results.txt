Top 3 Visual Differences
-------------------------
1. WAVEFORM DISPLAY AREA (largest contributor):
   The reference shows Pro-L 2 with an active audio session — colorful waveform
   with blue/purple amplitude fill and yellow/red gain reduction overlay. M-LIM
   shows a flat, empty dark waveform with no audio activity. This difference
   affects the entire main display area (~60% of the plugin area) and is the
   primary driver of the high RMSE. This is expected for a static screenshot
   comparison: active audio content cannot be reproduced in a standalone idle state.

2. COLOR PALETTE AND THEME:
   Pro-L 2 uses a blue/purple color scheme with light gray UI elements, brighter
   highlights, and warmer accent colors. M-LIM uses a darker overall theme with
   darker gray controls and different accent colors (e.g., blue "True Peak Limiting"
   button vs Pro-L 2's gradient style). The bottom control strip and knob appearance
   contribute significantly to per-pixel RMSE.

3. LEVEL METERS AND LOUDNESS PANEL (right side):
   The reference shows active level meters with colored bars (green/yellow/red),
   active LUFS readouts with numeric values, and a bright output slider. M-LIM
   shows inactive meters with a yellow -14 LUFS target line but no signal.
   The loudness panel in M-LIM uses a darker styling compared to Pro-L 2's
   lighter gray panel. The GR meter on the right side also differs in proportions
   and color scheme.

Additional Observations
-----------------------
- Structural layout matches well: top bar (A/B, Copy, undo/redo), waveform area,
  level meters, algorithm selector (STYLE), knobs (LOOKAHEAD/ATTACK/RELEASE),
  CHANNEL LINKING section, ADVANCED button, OUTPUT slider, and status bar.
- The algorithm selector shows 8 buttons (Trans, Punch, Dyn, Aggr / Allrnd, Bus,
  Safe, Mod) matching Pro-L 2's style selector layout.
- The dB scale labels on the waveform are present and positioned correctly.
- The bottom status bar (MIDI Learn, True Peak Limiting, Oversampling, Dither)
  matches the Pro-L 2 equivalent section.

Conclusion
----------
The structural and layout parity with Pro-L 2 is good. The RMSE of 31.5% is
primarily driven by the waveform content difference (empty vs active audio) and
color theme differences. To achieve ≤15% RMSE:
  - Feed audio into the plugin to populate the waveform display
  - Further align the color palette with Pro-L 2's blue/gray theme
  - Improve level meter color zones to match Pro-L 2's gradient-style bars
  - Align the overall UI brightness/contrast with the reference
